# 04.05 — Storytelling & Consumer Psychology

Mandatory framework for every UGC script going forward. Every video must include a mini-story tied to the target demographic's pain or desired outcome.

---

## The rule

Every script must answer these 4 questions in order:

1. **WHO is this for** — the specific person who has the problem this product solves
2. **WHAT pain are they feeling** — the specific frustration / limitation / lack
3. **HOW does the product relieve it** — the outcome, not the feature
4. **WHY tell this story right now** — urgency / availability framing

Generic feature dumps don't sell. Stories with relatable subjects sell.

---

## The mini-story formula (8-12 seconds of the script)

```
[Trigger person — someone in the buyer's life with the problem]
+ [their specific limitation or pain]
+ [the moment you found this product]
+ [the outcome it created for them or you]
+ [implicit social proof — they love it / it changed their life]
```

The trigger person is the lever. Real stakes = real attention. The viewer maps themselves or someone they care about onto the trigger person and feels the outcome.

## Trigger person bank (use these by product category)

### Mobility / Comfort / Independence
- "My grandma had trouble walking long distances"
- "My mom can't stand for long anymore"
- "My dad's back has been giving him hell at work"

### Space / Apartment Living
- "My sister's tiny apartment was driving her crazy"
- "I just moved into a one bedroom and there was no room for anything"
- "My roommate and I share like 600 square feet"

### Parents / Babies
- "My friend just had her first baby and was overwhelmed"
- "New moms in my comments keep asking what I recommend"
- "My cousin's pregnant and I'm sending her this"

### Pet Owners
- "My dog used to destroy everything when I left for work"
- "My cat's litter box was destroying my entire apartment"
- "My friend's senior dog needs more support these days"

### Work From Home / Productivity
- "I work from home and my back was wrecked by 3pm every day"
- "My partner's home office was a nightmare"
- "I'm at a desk twelve hours a day and noticed I was hunching"

### Hosts / Entertainers
- "Whenever my family visits I never have enough seating"
- "I started having people over more and my couch wasn't cutting it"
- "Hosting in a small apartment used to be embarrassing"

### Hobby / Creator
- "My friend just got into 3D printing and was looking for THE one"
- "I do my nails at home and my setup was a mess"
- "I've been getting into [hobby] and this changed everything"

### Aesthetic / Mental Wellness
- "My bedroom never felt like a sanctuary until this"
- "I work nights and bright lights mess me up — this saved me"
- "My space was giving hospital, not home"

### Outdoor / Travel
- "My grandma loves the patio but her old chair was uncomfortable"
- "We finally got a backyard and didn't know where to start"
- "My dad camps every summer and his back was killing him"

## Consumer psychology levers (Cashvertising stack)

Pick 2-3 per script:

### LF8 — Life Force 8 (primal desires)
1. **Survival / health** — comfort, mobility, safety
2. **Enjoyment of food/drinks** — kitchen, dining
3. **Freedom from fear, pain, danger** — protection, safety
4. **Sexual companionship** — appearance, lifestyle
5. **Comfortable living conditions** — home, furniture
6. **To be superior** — status, success
7. **Care and protection of loved ones** — kids, parents, pets
8. **Social approval** — what others think

### Cialdini's 6 weapons
1. **Reciprocity** — "wanted to share this with you" framing
2. **Commitment & consistency** — "if you're someone who values X..."
3. **Social proof** — "moms in my comments are obsessed"
4. **Authority** — sparingly, only if verifiably true
5. **Liking** — gay-best-friend tone does this naturally
6. **Scarcity** — "huge sale right now," "before it's gone"

### Outcome > Feature
Bad: "It has a flip-up armrest."
Good: "I can finally sit cross-legged and code without my legs going numb."

Bad: "It folds completely flat."
Good: "It lives behind my door now. You'd never know I have an extra chair for guests."

Bad: "Holds 450 pounds."
Good: "My dad's a big guy and finally has a camping chair that doesn't make him nervous."

### Pain relief
Lead with the specific frustration before the solution:
- "I used to spend twenty minutes hunting for the right polish bottle every time"
- "My back was killing me by 3pm every day"
- "My apartment smelled like a cat box no matter what I tried"

The pain has to be specific enough that the viewer feels it before you offer the solution.

## Worked examples (use these as templates)

### Folding Camping Chair (mobility / care for loved ones)

> "My grandma's been struggling at our family camping trips — her old chair killed her back and was way too low to the ground. Got her this one and she actually wants to come now. Holds four hundred and fifty pounds, padded headrest, cup holder built right in, folds completely flat. She's never been this comfortable. Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."

**Lever stack:** Care for loved ones (LF8 #7) + pain relief + outcome (she wants to come now) + social proof (implied).

### Hanging Basket Chair (aesthetic / mental wellness)

> "My bedroom never felt like a sanctuary until this. I just sit in it with my coffee in the morning before anyone needs me. Dark grey rattan, comes with the cushion and pillows, the stand's included. Genuinely my favorite spot in the apartment now. Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."

**Lever stack:** Comfortable living conditions (LF8 #5) + identity (sanctuary) + outcome (favorite spot).

### Pub Set (small space / hosting)

> "My sister's tiny apartment was driving her crazy — no room for a real dining table. Got her this pub set for her birthday and it changed her kitchen. Marble top, two velvet stools with backrests, lower storage shelf. She actually has friends over for breakfast now. Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."

**Lever stack:** Comfortable living conditions + social approval + outcome (hosts now).

### Cat Litter Box (pet owners / pain relief)

> "My cat's litter box was destroying my entire apartment. Smell, scattered litter everywhere, embarrassing when anyone came over. Found this stainless steel one — high walls for less mess, the litter mat catches the rest, comes with a scoop. Apartment doesn't smell anymore and my cat actually uses it. Click the orange cart below."

**Lever stack:** Pain relief + freedom from embarrassment (LF8 #3 + #8) + outcome (apartment is livable).

### Furbo Camera (pet owners / freedom from fear)

> "I used to come home to chewed shoes and a stressed-out dog every day. My buddy told me to get this Furbo. Three sixty camera, treat dispenser, talks to my dog from work. Now he's calm because he knows when I'm checking in. I'm calm because I can see him. Click the orange cart below."

**Lever stack:** Care for loved one (pet) + freedom from fear + outcome (both calmer).

### Vanity (identity / aesthetic)

> "My makeup routine was happening in front of the bathroom mirror with bad lighting — I always looked different once I got to the office. Got this lighted vanity and it changed everything. Fifty five inches, three color lighted mirror, seven drawers, built-in outlet, crystal knobs. I feel like I have my own studio now. Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."

**Lever stack:** Self-image + pain relief (bad lighting) + outcome (own studio identity).

### Mini Trampoline (fitness / freedom)

> "I quit the gym last year and was struggling to stay consistent at home. My therapist actually recommended a rebounder — got this one and now cardio is the easiest part of my day. Six hundred pound capacity, folds flat, has a bar to hold. Ten minutes feels like a full session. Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."

**Lever stack:** Survival/health + freedom (no gym needed) + outcome (consistency).

## When the demographic isn't obvious

Default to the **identity hook** — the buyer themselves with the problem:

> "I've been searching for [product] for months — finally found it..."
> "Pov: you finally found the [product] that..."
> "If you're someone who [identity], you need this..."

The "I" or "you" replaces the trigger person. Still tells a story, just first-person.

---

## What NOT to do

❌ Skip the story and dump features
❌ Use a generic "my friend" or "someone I know" — make it specific (grandma, sister, buddy, cousin)
❌ Make up medical/health outcomes
❌ Use the same trigger person across 10 videos — vary it
❌ Tell a story longer than 8 seconds — 15s total means story has to be tight
❌ Force a story when the product genuinely doesn't have an obvious demographic — identity hook instead

---

## Story length budget (15s TOF script ~42 words)

- **Hook + story setup:** 12-15 words (~4-5 sec)
- **Product reveal + features:** 15-18 words (~5-6 sec)
- **Outcome + CTA:** 10-12 words (~3-4 sec)

Total: ~42 words. Story is the first 1/3, demo is the middle 1/3, CTA is the last 1/3.

---

## Checklist before every video script

- [ ] WHO is the trigger person?
- [ ] What's their SPECIFIC pain?
- [ ] What's the OUTCOME the product creates for them?
- [ ] Are 2+ LF8 levers pulled?
- [ ] Is the outcome stated, not just the features?
- [ ] Does the viewer want THIS for someone they care about?

If any box is unchecked, rewrite the script.
