# 04.02 — Hook Frameworks

The first 1-2 seconds of a TikTok determine whether the viewer stays or scrolls. Hooks are the most important sentence in the script.

---

## The 6 hook families

### 1. Surprise-praise
"Yo whoever priced this needs a raise."
"They're losing money on this."
"Someone bullied the price down on this."

**Why it works:** Praises a deal without naming the price. Triggers curiosity about HOW good the price is.

### 2. Stop command
"Okay STOP — y'all need to see this."
"Wait wait wait —"
"Pause —"
"Hold up —"

**Why it works:** Verbal interrupt mirrors the scroll-stop motion. Reader's thumb hovers.

### 3. Curiosity loop
"Tell me why nobody talks about this."
"Why is nobody buying this right now?"
"The [product] nobody is talking about."
"This one's actually crazy and nobody's noticed."

**Why it works:** Implies hidden gem. Triggers FOMO.

### 4. Reaction trigger
"I cannot BELIEVE this."
"I am genuinely losing it."
"Wait this is unserious."
"This is so wrong for the price."

**Why it works:** Borrows reaction from the creator. Viewer wants to know what's so shocking.

### 5. Identity hook
"If you're the type of person who [X]..."
"Pov: you walked into [scene] and saw this."
"This is for the girlies who [X]."
"This is for the men who [X]."

**Why it works:** Self-identification. Viewer locks in if they match the identity.

### 6. Mini-story open
"Okay so I was looking for [X]..."
"Bro look at what I found."
"I have been searching for [X] for months..."

**Why it works:** Personal narrative bridges to trust. But: must be brief or feels scripted.

---

## Hook A/B testing

For high-priority products, generate 3 versions with different hook families:

1. Stop command
2. Surprise-praise
3. Curiosity loop

Post all 3 on different days/accounts. Track 24h view performance. Use the winner as the template for that product's MOF/BOF videos.

---

## Hook length

15 second TOF videos: hook = 1-3 seconds (5-8 words).

Examples by length:

| Length | Hook |
|---|---|
| 2 words | "Pause —" |
| 3 words | "Wait wait wait —" |
| 5 words | "Okay STOP — y'all need to see this." |
| 8 words | "Tell me why nobody talks about this vanity." |
| 10 words | "Yo whoever priced this fireplace TV stand needs a raise." |

Anything over 10 words for the hook is too long — you're already into the body of the script.

---

## Hook → body transition

The hook should make the viewer ask "what?" or "why?" — the body answers.

Hook: "Tell me why nobody talks about this vanity."
Body: "Hollywood lights all the way around, seven drawers, bench included."

Hook: "Okay STOP — y'all need to see this chair."
Body: "It folds, it's real acacia wood, and it holds like four hundred pounds."

The body must immediately reward the curiosity the hook generated. Don't bury the answer.

---

## Banned hook patterns

### "Let me show you..."
- Reads as scripted ad
- Drops retention by 30%+

### "Today I'm going to..."
- Same

### "Hey guys / hi guys / what's up"
- Generic
- Signals "creator account" not authentic discovery

### "If you're in the market for..."
- Sales-y

### "Are you tired of..."
- Infomercial cliche

### "Have you been looking for..."
- Same

### "Let me tell you about..."
- Same

These hooks signal "this is an ad" before the viewer even gets to the product. Avoid all of them.

---

## Voice and pacing tells

For the gay-best-friend voice, hooks should:
- Have a slight pitch lift at the end (sets up the body)
- Lead with a punchy consonant (S, P, W, K, Y all good frame-1 starters)
- Avoid soft vowels at the start (A, I, U sound flat in Kling's frame 0)
