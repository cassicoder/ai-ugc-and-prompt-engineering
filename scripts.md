# Scripts

TikTok Shop scripting for affiliate UGC. Frameworks, storytelling, compliance, examples — one file.

---

## The 15-second TOF budget

A 15-second top-of-funnel script is approximately **42 words**, broken into three 1/3 chunks:

| Chunk | Beat | Words |
|---|---|---|
| 0–5s | Trigger person + pain | 12–15 |
| 5–10s | Product reveal + features | 15–18 |
| 10–15s | Outcome + CTA | 10–12 |

Story is the first third. Demo is the middle third. CTA is the last third.

---

## Storytelling — the mandatory framework

Every script answers these four questions in order:

1. **WHO** is this for — the specific person who has the problem
2. **WHAT** pain are they feeling — the specific frustration
3. **HOW** does the product relieve it — the outcome, not the feature
4. **WHY** tell this story right now — urgency or availability framing

Generic feature dumps don't sell. Stories with relatable subjects sell.

### The mini-story formula (8–12 seconds of the script)

```
[Trigger person — someone in the buyer's life with the problem]
+ [their specific limitation or pain]
+ [the moment you found this product]
+ [the outcome it created for them or you]
+ [implicit social proof — they love it / it changed their life]
```

The trigger person is the lever. Real stakes = real attention.

### Trigger person bank by product category

**Mobility / comfort / independence**
- "My grandma had trouble walking long distances"
- "My mom can't stand for long anymore"
- "My dad's back has been giving him hell at work"

**Space / apartment living**
- "My sister's tiny apartment was driving her crazy"
- "I just moved into a one bedroom and there was no room"
- "My roommate and I share like 600 square feet"

**Parents / babies**
- "My friend just had her first baby and was overwhelmed"
- "New moms in my comments keep asking what I recommend"
- "My cousin's pregnant and I'm sending her this"

**Pet owners**
- "My dog used to destroy everything when I left for work"
- "My cat's litter box was destroying my entire apartment"
- "My friend's senior dog needs more support these days"

**Work from home / productivity**
- "I work from home and my back was wrecked by 3pm every day"
- "I'm at a desk twelve hours a day and noticed I was hunching"

**Hosts / entertainers**
- "Whenever my family visits I never have enough seating"
- "I started having people over more and my couch wasn't cutting it"

**Hobby / creator**
- "My friend just got into 3D printing and was looking for THE one"
- "I do my nails at home and my setup was a mess"

**Aesthetic / mental wellness**
- "My bedroom never felt like a sanctuary until this"
- "My space was giving hospital, not home"

**Outdoor / travel**
- "My grandma loves the patio but her old chair was uncomfortable"
- "We finally got a backyard and didn't know where to start"

**Identity hook (when no trigger person fits)**
- "I've been searching for [product] for months — finally found it"
- "Pov: you finally found the [product] that..."
- "If you're someone who [identity], you need this"

## Consumer psychology levers (Cashvertising stack)

Pick 2–3 per script.

### LF8 — Life Force 8 primal desires

1. Survival / health — comfort, mobility, safety
2. Enjoyment of food / drinks
3. Freedom from fear, pain, danger
4. Sexual companionship — appearance, lifestyle
5. Comfortable living conditions — home, furniture
6. To be superior — status, success
7. Care and protection of loved ones — kids, parents, pets
8. Social approval — what others think

### Cialdini's 6 weapons

1. **Reciprocity** — "wanted to share this with you"
2. **Commitment & consistency** — "if you're someone who values X..."
3. **Social proof** — "moms in my comments are obsessed"
4. **Authority** — sparingly, only if verifiably true
5. **Liking** — the gay-best-friend voice does this naturally
6. **Scarcity** — "huge sale right now," "before it's gone"

### Outcome > feature

Bad: "It has a flip-up armrest."
Good: "I can finally sit cross-legged and code without my legs going numb."

Bad: "Holds 450 pounds."
Good: "My dad's a big guy and finally has a camping chair that doesn't make him nervous."

### Pain relief — lead with specific frustration

- "I used to spend twenty minutes hunting for the right polish bottle every time"
- "My back was killing me by 3pm every day"
- "My apartment smelled like a cat box no matter what I tried"

## Hook frameworks

The first 1.5 seconds. If a hook doesn't earn the next second, the rest doesn't matter.

### Hook types that work

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Trigger person | "My grandma had trouble walking long distances..." |
| Identity | "Pov: you finally found the perfect..." |
| Specific frustration | "I used to spend twenty minutes hunting for the right..." |
| Discovery | "I just found the wildest..." |
| Question | "Why does no one talk about this..." |
| Stat (when verifiable) | "16K people bought this last month..." |

### Hook patterns that fail

- Generic announcement: "Check out this product"
- Vague claim: "This is amazing"
- Lifestyle filler: "It's a beautiful day and I wanted to share..."

### Hook test

If you read the hook out loud and your own response is "so what," rewrite it. The hook either creates **curiosity, identification,** or **specific pain recognition** in the first 1.5 seconds, or it's dead.

## TOF / MOF / BOF

| Stage | Goal | $ allowed | Tone | CTA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TOF (top of funnel) | Stop the scroll, build curiosity | No $ amounts, no % off | "Huge sale" / "before it's gone" | `click the orange cart below` |
| MOF (middle of funnel) | Build trust + demonstrate | Vague price OK ("under $100") | Feature breakdown, demo | `click the orange cart below` |
| BOF (bottom of funnel) | Convert | Specific price + discount OK | Direct comparison, urgency | `click the orange cart below` |

**TOF locked rule: never use dollar amounts or percent off.** Replace with `huge sale`, `crazy deal right now`, `before it's gone`.

---

## CTA — locked

```
click the orange cart below
```

In-app cart icon = safe. Off-platform routing (`DM me`, `link in bio`, `click my profile`) risks flagging.

Variations:
- "Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."
- "Tap the orange cart below before this sells out."
- "Click the orange cart below — it goes fast."

If Kling can't say "orange" cleanly even with the speaker-tag reinforcement, replace with: `tap the cart below` or `click the cart icon below`.

## Compliance

### CHR — the scoring system

- Starts at 200 / 1000 for new accounts
- Each violation drops the score
- Six strikes of the same violation type in 90 days = permanent removal

**One wrong claim per 30–50 videos is the ceiling.**

### Forbidden claim categories

**Medical / health**
- "Cures" / "treats" / "heals"
- "FDA approved" (unless literally true)
- "Doctor recommended"
- "Medical-grade"
- Curing specific conditions
- Anything implying medical efficacy

**Disparaging competitors by name**
- Costco, Wayfair, Amazon, Target, Walmart, HomeGoods — never named
- "Way cheaper than [brand]" — use `the home store` / `online` / `the other place`

**False scarcity**
- "Only 3 left" if there are 300
- "Sale ends tonight" if it doesn't

**Misleading specs**
- Claiming features that don't exist
- Claiming dimensions / weight / capacity not in listing
- Claiming materials that aren't real (e.g. "solid wood" for wood-look)

**Whole-room or extreme utility**
- "Heats the entire room" for accent fireplaces
- "Burns 500 calories" for a piece of fitness gear

**Specific weight loss numbers**
- "I lost 15 pounds with this" — forbidden

**Health benefit claims**
- "Boosts immunity" — forbidden
- "Better sleep" as outcome — forbidden
- "I sleep better with this" as subjective experience — allowed

**The line:** subjective sensory claims allowed; specific health outcomes forbidden.

| OK | Not OK |
|---|---|
| "Makes my teeth feel cleaner" | "Whitens teeth in 3 days" |
| "I sleep better with this" | "Cures insomnia" |
| "Feels relaxing" | "Reduces stress" |

### Trigger person framing — is it a violation?

**No.** "My grandma," "my mom," "my friend" framings are standard UGC convention and accepted by FTC and TikTok as illustrative storytelling.

The line is in the **claim**, not the **character**.

| Safe | Risk |
|---|---|
| "My grandma had trouble walking long distances" | "My grandma was prescribed this by her doctor" |
| "I got my mom this for her birthday" | "Her arthritis is gone since using this" |
| "My dad's back gives him hell at work" | "Her doctor couldn't help but this did" |
| "She's way more comfortable now" | "She lost 15 pounds with this" |

### Required actions

- **AI label:** every AI-generated video must have the AI label toggled ON at upload. Not optional.
- **Real product:** the product shown must match what's sold. No bait-and-switch.

### Categories — risk levels

| Category | Risk | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Furniture (large) | ✅ Low | Stay on visible features, no material overclaim |
| Pet products | ✅ Low | Avoid "trains your pet to..." |
| Outdoor | ✅ Low | Stay on visible features |
| Bedding / mattress | ⚠️ Medium | "Better sleep" is a health claim — use sensory language |
| Kitchen | ⚠️ Medium | Food contact safety claims are regulated |
| Fitness | ⚠️ Medium | No weight loss numbers, no body claims |
| Baby | 🔴 High | Safety claims heavily regulated — stay on visible features + stated specs only |
| Health / beauty | 🔴 High | Use sensory language only, never efficacy |

### Pre-publish checklist

- [ ] Every spec claim verified visible in image OR in listing title
- [ ] No medical / health claims
- [ ] No competitor brand names
- [ ] AI label toggled ON
- [ ] CTA is "orange cart below"
- [ ] TOF: no $ or % amounts
- [ ] On-screen text under 8 words
- [ ] Captions and on-screen text aligned with video claims

### Violation playbook

If a video gets flagged:
1. Don't delete immediately — deletion reads as admission
2. Read the violation reason carefully
3. Submit appeal within 24 hours if the flag seems wrong
4. Pull the video if the flag is correct
5. Audit the next 5 videos in the queue for the same pattern
6. Add the pattern to `failure-modes.md`

Standing appeal template:
> "This video accurately represents the product as listed in the [seller name] product page. The features mentioned ([list]) are explicitly stated in the product title and visible in the seller's primary product image. Requesting review."

### The lawyer test

Before saying anything in a script, ask:

> If a lawyer reviewed this claim, could the product be sued for not delivering?

If yes, soften or remove.

| OK | Not OK |
|---|---|
| "I'm obsessed with how this feels" | "This will fix your X" |
| "Looks insanely real" | "Indistinguishable from real X" |
| "Makes my [thing] feel [sensory adjective]" | "Makes my [thing] [measurable improvement]" |

## Worked script examples

### Folding camping chair (mobility / care for loved ones)

> "My grandma's been struggling at our family camping trips — her old chair killed her back and was way too low to the ground. Got her this one and she actually wants to come now. Holds four hundred and fifty pounds, padded headrest, cup holder built right in, folds completely flat. She's never been this comfortable. Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."

Lever stack: care for loved ones (LF8 #7) + pain relief + outcome (she wants to come now) + implied social proof.

### Hanging basket chair (aesthetic / mental wellness)

> "My bedroom never felt like a sanctuary until this. I just sit in it with my coffee in the morning before anyone needs me. Dark grey rattan, comes with the cushion and pillows, the stand's included. Genuinely my favorite spot in the apartment now. Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."

Lever stack: comfortable living conditions (LF8 #5) + identity (sanctuary) + outcome (favorite spot).

### Pub set (small space / hosting)

> "My sister's tiny apartment was driving her crazy — no room for a real dining table. Got her this pub set for her birthday and it changed her kitchen. Marble top, two velvet stools with backrests, lower storage shelf. She actually has friends over for breakfast now. Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."

Lever stack: comfortable living + social approval + outcome (hosts now).

### Cat litter box (pet owners / pain relief)

> "My cat's litter box was destroying my entire apartment. Smell, scattered litter everywhere, embarrassing when anyone came over. Found this stainless steel one — high walls for less mess, the litter mat catches the rest, comes with a scoop. Apartment doesn't smell anymore and my cat actually uses it. Click the orange cart below."

Lever stack: pain relief + freedom from embarrassment (LF8 #3 + #8) + outcome (apartment livable).

### Mini trampoline (fitness / freedom)

> "I quit the gym last year and was struggling to stay consistent at home. My therapist actually recommended a rebounder — got this one and now cardio is the easiest part of my day. Six hundred pound capacity, folds flat, has a bar to hold. Ten minutes feels like a full session. Huge sale right now, click the orange cart below."

Lever stack: survival/health + freedom (no gym needed) + outcome (consistency).
